History

Government College, Bida, established in 1912 (with first intake in 1914), is a historic Nigerian boys' boarding school known for producing many prominent figures, including two Nigerian Heads of State, governors, and ministers, evolving through names like Bida Middle School and Niger Provincial Secondary School before becoming Government College, Bida in 1970.

Prominent Graduates

The most prominent graduates of Government College Bida, one of Nigeria's historic secondary school known for producing influencial leaders accross many fields, to mention but few.

Gen. Ibrahim Badamasi Babangida CGFR

Former Nigeria Military Head of State (1985-1993). One of the most internationally known alumni of the school. Born in northern Niger state, Babangida received military training in Nigeria, India, Great Britain, and the United States. He rose through the ranks and was known for his courage—he played a major role in suppressing an attempted coup in 1976 when he walked into a rebel-held radio station unarmed.

Gen. Abdulsalam Abubakar CGFR

Abdusalam Abubakar (born June 13, 1942, Minna, Nigeria) is a Nigerian military leader, who served as head of state (1998–99). Also one of the most internationally known alumni of the school. Hailing from the middle belt of the country, Abubakar joined the army in 1975 and received his formal military training in the United States. He commanded Nigeria’s contingent of United Nations peacekeeping troops in Lebanon in 1981.

Professor A. A. Zuru

Professor Abdullahi Abdul Zuru is a prominent figure in Nigeria's academic and professional landscape, also Almunus of Government College Bida. He served as the Vice Chancellor at Usmanu Danfodiyo University, Sokoto, and Kebbi State University. His contributions to science and technology were recognized, and he was involved in various research and development projects.
